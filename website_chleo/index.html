About

I am a PhD Candidate in Economics at IÉSEG School of Management and LEM-CNRS 9221, Lille, and a member of the IFLAME research center (IÉSEG Research Center on Family, Labor and Migration Economics). I hold a Master's degree in Analysis and Policy in Economics from the Paris School of Economics and a dual Master's degree in Economic and Social History from University Paris 1 Panthéon-Sorbonne, as well as a dual Bachelor's degree in Economics and History from Paris 1.

My research uses historical micro-data to study the long-run determinants of economic growth, with a focus on demography, migration, and education in European history. My PhD thesis, Demography, Migration, and Education: Micro-foundations of European Long-Run Growth, is supervised by Thomas Baudin and Simone Moriconi, as a part of the FaMiGrowth Research Project.

Research

Thesis

Demography, Migration, and Education: Micro-foundations of European Long-Run Growth

PhD Thesis · IÉSEG School of Management & LEM-CNRS 9221 · Since 2025

This thesis investigates the micro-level determinants of European long-run economic growth through the lens of historical demography, migration, and human capital formation, using archival and genealogical micro-data.

Economic History Historical Demography Migration Human Capital

Master's Research

A Financial Revolution in Education: The Case of Parisian 'Petites Écoles' (1789–1797)

Master's thesis · Paris School of Economics · 2025

Examines the financing of elementary schooling in Paris during the early French Revolution, tracing how the disruption of traditional funding sources reshaped access to primary education.

Economic History Economics of Education French Revolution Historical Demography Human Capital Public Policy

Le financement des politiques éducatives au début de la Révolution française : étude de cas des petites écoles de Paris (1789–1797)

Master's thesis · University Paris 1 Panthéon-Sorbonne · 2025

French-language companion thesis exploring the same episode from a social and cultural history perspective, focusing on the institutional and political dimensions of educational financing under the Revolution.
